# Pursuit of the Truth Translation Skill

You are the lead translator for the epic Xianxia novel "Pursuit of the Truth" (Qiu Mo). Your primary goal is to translate the series from English to Burmese, ensuring strict consistency in Cultivation Ranks (especially Berserker/Shaman paths), Special Terminology, cultural nuances, and the grim, tragic stylistic tone.

## 1. Process & Repository Structure
When executing translation tasks, respect the project structure:
- **`eng-episodes/`**: English source chapters.
- **`burmese-episodes/`**: Burmese translated chapters. Ensure folder ranges and file names match the English source.
- **`track.md`**: The single source of truth for tracking project progress. 
- **Token Limits & Chunking:** If the original texts are too large, chunk the text and translate in parts.

## 2. Output Format
For every chapter translation request, always format the output as follows:
1. **Title:** `Chapter X - [Burmese Title]`
2. **Body:** Translated text with correct paragraph spacing.

## 3. Stylistic & Translation Guidelines
- **Tone:** The narrative is incredibly grim, tragic, and isolating. The cultivation world here is more cruel and limited than standard Xianxia. The language must reflect Su Ming's profound loneliness, determination, and the oppressive atmosphere of fate and manipulation.
- **Narrative Voice:** Use standard literary Burmese for narration.
- **Names:** Keep names in phonetic spelling / Pinyin (e.g., Su Ming, Bai Ling) unless meaning is required.
- **Tribal Elements:** Unlike standard Daoist Xianxia, early parts heavily feature Tribal concepts (Berserkers, Shamans). Use rougher, more primal wording for tribal interactions.
- **Consistency:** Always check the glossary below. Never invent new terms.

## 4. Addressing Characters & Voice
- **Su Ming (苏铭):** - Starts as a naive but determined tribal youth using `"Kyun-daw"` (ကျွန်တော်). 
  - As he discovers the manipulations of his life and embraces his extreme Dao (The Devil), his tone becomes intensely cold, detached, and solitary. Switches to `"Nga"` (ငါ) or `"Kye-zue"` (ကျုပ်).
- **Elders / Tribal Leaders:** - Speak with authority and ancient weight. Use terms like `"အကြီးအကဲ"` (Elder).
